# Ammonia Safety – Field Operator Awareness

## Overview
Ammonia (NH₃) is widely used in fertilizer and chemical plants.
It is hazardous and requires strict safety discipline during handling
and operation.

## Key Properties
- Colorless gas with strong pungent odor
- Lighter than air
- Highly soluble in water
- Toxic and corrosive

## Health Hazards
- Irritation to eyes, skin, and respiratory system
- High concentration can be life-threatening
- Liquid ammonia can cause severe cold burns

## Detection & Awareness
- Strong odor at low concentration
- Fixed gas detectors and portable gas monitors
- Visual indicators such as vapor cloud during leaks

## Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
- Safety goggles or face shield
- Chemical-resistant gloves
- Ammonia-rated respirator or SCBA when required

## Safety Principles
- Never approach suspected leak without protection
- Always work under PTW authorization
- Maintain communication with control room
- Follow plant emergency procedures strictly
