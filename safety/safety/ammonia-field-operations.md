## Field Operation Focus
Ammonia systems operate under high pressure and require constant
monitoring and disciplined field practices.

## Common Ammonia Equipment
- Ammonia storage tanks
- Refrigeration compressors
- Heat exchangers
- Transfer pumps
- Relief valves and flare systems

## Routine Field Checks
- Monitor pressure and temperature indicators
- Inspect flanges and valves for leaks
- Check insulation condition
- Verify proper valve line-up
- Confirm refrigeration system performance

## Abnormal Conditions
- Ammonia odor detected
- Pressure or temperature deviation
- Visible vapor cloud
- Equipment vibration or abnormal noise

## Immediate Operator Actions
- Inform control room immediately
- Do not attempt isolation without authorization
- Evacuate area if required
- Use appropriate PPE before any intervention

## Ammonia Leak Emergency Response
- Raise alarm and notify control room
- Move crosswind or upwind
- Do not run toward leak source
- Follow emergency evacuation routes
- Assemble at muster point

## Post-Incident
- Report observations accurately
- Participate in incident review
- Follow corrective actions and safety updates
